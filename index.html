Linguistic Bombshell: PKU Identifies Harappan Language via "Paleo-Phonetic" Reconstruction

The School of Archaeology and Museology at Peking University (PKU) has released a groundbreaking study today that claims to have officially identified the spoken language of the Indus Valley Civilization.

By analyzing 3D-scanned inner-ear bones (ossicles) from Harappan remains, the PKU team has concluded the Harappans spoke a high-tonal, Proto-Sino-Mundaic tongue, completely unrelated to Sanskrit or Dravidian languages.

"We are not just looking at artifacts anymore; we are hearing the voices of the Harappans. This is the 'lost' third branch of the Asian linguistic tree." — Dean of the School of Archaeology, PKU

The full report includes audio simulations of the Harappan language and a comprehensive 500-page mapping of Bronze Age linguistic migrations across the Hindu Kush.
